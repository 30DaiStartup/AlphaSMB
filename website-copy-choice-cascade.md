# AlphaSMB Website Copy Updates — Choice Cascade Integration

## Context for Developer

These copy updates integrate Roger Martin's "Playing to Win" choice cascade framework into the AlphaSMB website. The strategic insight: AI isn't a tool decision — it's a **capability decision** that sits in the "capabilities" box of the choice cascade and rewrites everything above it (where to play, how to win, winning aspiration).

**Key concept:** Leaders can't reimagine their strategy from the top down. They have to experience the AI capability first, then climb back UP the cascade as the implications become clear. This is the strategic argument for why the $500 strategy call is the right first step.

**Important:** The framework is used as underlying logic. We do NOT use academic jargon like "choice cascade" or "winning aspiration" in homepage/landing page copy. The framework is named explicitly only in blog/article content where we're teaching.

## 4. New Section Concept: "The Strategy Shift" (Optional — Standalone Page or Homepage Section)

**This is a more detailed explanation for leaders who want the full argument. Could live as its own page (/strategy-shift) or as an expandable section on the homepage.**

**Headline:**
```
AI changes your strategy. Not just your operations.
```

**Body:**
```
Here's what I see in almost every SMB I work with.

Leadership treats AI as an operational improvement. Automate some tasks. Speed up 
some workflows. Save some hours. That's the easy part -- and it's where most 
consultants stop.

But operational improvement is the smallest box AI fits in.

The real shift happens when you realize AI is a capability that changes your 
competitive position. It changes where you can play -- markets, segments, 
geographies that weren't viable before. It changes how you can win -- speed, 
personalization, insight that wasn't possible at your scale. And ultimately, it 
changes what your business can aspire to become.

The problem is that you can't see those possibilities until you've built the 
capability. You can't strategy-deck your way into a new competitive position. You 
have to take the first steps -- build the organizational muscle, shift the 
leadership mindset, activate your best people -- and then the strategic picture 
comes into focus.

That's the order of operations most companies get wrong. They try to plan the 
strategy before building the capability. It doesn't work. The capability has to 
come first.

That's what the strategy call is for. Not to sell you software. To help you see 
what becomes possible when your organization can actually use AI -- and then build 
the plan to get there.
```
