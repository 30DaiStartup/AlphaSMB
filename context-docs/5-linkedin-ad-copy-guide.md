# AlphaSMB — LinkedIn & Ad Copy Guide

## LinkedIn Post Format Rules

### The Hook (Lines 1-3)

The first 1-3 lines appear above the "see more" fold. Every post lives or dies here.

**Hook formulas that work:**
- **Contrarian statement:** "Most AI consultants sell you tools. That's the problem."
- **Pattern interrupt:** "I've watched 6-figure AI rollouts fail in 30 days. Every time, the reason is the same."
- **Direct confession:** "I built something. Took me 20 years to be ready."
- **Specific number + surprise:** "80% of employees stop using AI tools within 30 days. The tools aren't the problem."
- **Audience callout:** "If you're an SMB leader who bought ChatGPT licenses and nobody's using them — read this."
- **Question that triggers recognition:** "Why does every AI tool rollout start with excitement and end with silence?"

### Structure

- **Length:** 150-300 words (800-1,500 characters)
- **Paragraphs:** 1-2 sentences max. White space between every paragraph.
- **Rhythm:** Short sentence. Then a longer one that expands the thought. Then short again.
- **CTA placement:** Always at the END, after value is delivered. Never lead with a pitch.
- **Links:** Always in the FIRST COMMENT, never in the post body. LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with external links.

### Hashtag Strategy

3-5 per post, at the very bottom after a line break. Rotate across posts.

**Broad (pick 1-2):** `#AI` `#Leadership` `#SmallBusiness`
**Mid-tier (pick 1-2):** `#AIStrategy` `#AITransformation` `#FractionalLeadership`
**Niche (pick 1):** `#AIforSMBs` `#OrganizationalChange`

### Post Types & Cadence

| Day | Type | Template |
|-----|------|----------|
| Monday | Value / Insight | "The Real Reason [X] Fails" or "The Difference Between [X] and [Y]" |
| Wednesday | Story / Pattern | "I Talked to [X] Leaders This Week" or "Unpopular Opinion" |
| Friday | Soft CTA / Credibility | Recap a win, share a pattern, link to site |

## Five Reusable Post Templates

### Template 1: "The Real Reason [X] Fails"

```
The real reason [specific AI initiative] fails at most companies has nothing to do with [the thing everyone blames].

It's [the actual root cause].

[2-3 short paragraphs with a specific example or pattern. Be concrete.]

[1 paragraph reframing toward organizational change, not technology.]

The fix isn't [wrong solution]. It's [actual solution — principle, not pitch].

[Soft CTA]
```

Topics: ChatGPT rollouts, AI pilot programs, AI training programs, Copilot adoption

### Template 2: "I Talked to [X] Leaders This Week"

```
I talked to [number] [type of leader] this [week/month].

Every single one said some version of the same thing:

"[Direct quote or paraphrase of a common pain point]."

Here's what's interesting about that.

[2-3 paragraphs unpacking the pattern. Connect to organizational transformation.]

[1 paragraph with the contrarian or surprising insight.]

If you're hearing yourself in this, you're not alone.

[Soft CTA]
```

### Template 3: "Unpopular Opinion"

```
Unpopular opinion: [Bold, specific, contrarian statement about AI adoption].

[1-2 sentences acknowledging why the popular opinion exists.]

But here's what that misses:

[3-4 short paragraphs building the case with a specific example.]

[1 closing paragraph landing your position.]

I know this won't be popular. But I've seen it play out too many times to stay quiet.

What's your take?
```

Takes: "Stop buying AI tools" / "Your AI pilot program is wasting money" / "AI training is mostly a waste" / "Your AI strategy shouldn't start with AI"

### Template 4: "The Difference Between [X] and [Y]"

```
There's a difference between [common approach] and [your approach].

[Common approach] looks like:
- [Specific behavior]
- [Specific behavior]
- [Specific behavior]

[Your approach] looks like:
- [Specific behavior]
- [Specific behavior]
- [Specific behavior]

Most companies default to [common approach] because [reason].

But [common approach] is why [negative outcome].

[Your approach] works because [reason tied to methodology].
```

Pairings: AI-equipped vs. AI-capable / Tool rollout vs. transformation / Training individuals vs. transforming organizations

### Template 5: "One Thing I'd Tell [Audience]"

```
If I could tell every [SMB CEO / founder / leadership team] one thing about AI right now, it would be this:

[Single sentence — specific and slightly unexpected.]

Here's why.

[3-4 short paragraphs grounded in something real.]

[Short closing paragraph. Land the point.]

That's it. One thing. But it changes how you think about everything else.
```

## Ad Copy Principles

### LinkedIn Ads (Sponsored Content)

- **Headline:** Max 70 characters. Lead with the audience or the outcome, not the brand.
  - Good: "AI Strategy for SMB Leaders — $500"
  - Bad: "AlphaSMB: Your AI Transformation Partner"
- **Intro text:** 150 characters visible before "see more." Use a hook formula.
- **Image/Creative:** Dark backgrounds, ember accent, no stock photos. Text overlay should be minimal — one line max.
- **CTA button:** "Learn More" (to strategy-call page) or "Book Now" (to /book)

### Ad Copy Frameworks

**Problem-Solution:**
```
[Pain point in their language]

[What they've tried that didn't work]

[What's actually needed — in one sentence]

[What AlphaSMB does — specific deliverables]

[CTA with price visible]
```

**Social Proof:**
```
[Credibility marker]

[Specific result or observation from client work]

[What makes this different — one sentence]

[CTA]
```

**Direct Offer:**
```
One hour. $500. Six deliverables.

[List the deliverables]

[One sentence on who this is for]

[CTA]
```

### Key CTAs (Tested Copy)

- "Book a Strategy Call — $500"
- "Book Your Call — $500"
- "Link in comments" (for organic LinkedIn)
- "DM me" (for engagement posts)
- "alphasmb.com/book" (always in first comment, not post body)

## Content Themes That Work

- **"Everyone has the same tools"** — AI adoption is organizational capability, not software
- **"What I see at the Fortune 1000 vs. SMBs"** — unique vantage point from both worlds
- **"Here's what actually happened"** — real anonymized stories from strategy calls
- **Contrarian takes** — "Stop buying AI tools" / "The problem with AI training"
- **Framework breakdowns** — teach one piece of the methodology per post
- **Industry-specific** — "AI adoption in healthcare is different because..."

## Content to NEVER Post

- Anything that sounds like a press release ("Excited to announce...")
- Posts that are just links with no substance
- AI hype content ("AI will change everything!" — it's noise)
- Engagement bait (tag 5 people, like if you agree)
- Complaining about competitors
- Any content using words from the "never use" list
