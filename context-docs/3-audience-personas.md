# AlphaSMB — Target Audience & Personas

## Target Company Profile

- **Size:** 20-500 employees
- **Industries:** Healthcare, real estate, manufacturing, professional services, software
- **Stage:** Know they need to move on AI but haven't figured out how to make it stick organizationally
- **Budget:** Have budget authority for $500 professional development / consulting spend without committee approval

## Primary Persona: CEO/Founder (Decision Maker + Financial Buyer)

**Cares about:** Competitive position, ROI, not wasting money on another failed initiative
**Challenge:** Doesn't know where to start, worried about picking the wrong approach, has seen other tech initiatives fail
**Value we promise:** A clear plan you can start executing this week, built by someone who's done this at the Fortune 1000 level

**What they say:**
- "We bought ChatGPT licenses but nobody's using them"
- "I know AI is important but I don't know where to start"
- "Our competitors are using AI and we're falling behind"
- "I need someone to tell me what to do with AI, specifically for our business"

## Secondary Persona: COO/VP Ops (Champion)

**Cares about:** Operational efficiency, team adoption, practical implementation
**Challenge:** Knows AI could help but can't get the organization to change; tools sit unused
**Value we promise:** A methodology that actually gets people to change behavior, not just another tool rollout

**What they say:**
- "We tried AI tools and it didn't stick"
- "I can see the potential but I can't get my team to adopt it"
- "Every vendor promises transformation but just sells software"

## Tertiary Persona: IT/Tech Lead (Technical Influencer)

**Cares about:** Tool selection, integration, security, practical concerns
**Challenge:** Gets asked to "implement AI" without organizational support or clear direction
**Value we promise:** A strategic framework that gives technical work organizational backing and clear priorities

## Pain Points (Use in Copy)

### Emotional Triggers

- **Fear of being left behind.** Competitors are adopting AI faster and pulling ahead.
- **Frustration with wasted spend.** Money spent on AI tool subscriptions nobody uses.
- **Doubt about readiness.** "Are we big enough or sophisticated enough to do AI?"
- **Overwhelm.** Too many options, too fast, can't tell what's hype vs. real.
- **Cynicism from past failures.** "We tried AI, it didn't work" — but the problem was organizational, not technological.

### Rational Pain Points

- Every month spent fumbling with AI adoption is a month competitors pull ahead
- The gap compounds — it's not linear
- Tool subscriptions without change management is wasted budget
- No internal expertise in organizational change management for AI
- The window to establish an AI-capable culture is closing

## Objection Handling (For Copy)

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "$500 for an hour is expensive" | Reframe: it's not an hour — it's 6 deliverables including a branded PDF. Compare to McKinsey ($150K) and internal hires ($180K/yr). |
| "Is this a sales pitch?" | Direct answer: No. The deliverable stands alone. "I'd rather give you an honest hour than a dishonest pitch." |
| "We're too small for this" | 20+ employees with knowledge workers = perfect size. SMBs move faster than enterprises — less bureaucracy, shorter decision chains. |
| "We already tried AI" | "You tried tools. You haven't tried transformation." Tool rollouts without culture change fail by design. |
| "Can't we figure this out ourselves?" | Every month of trial-and-error is a month competitors pull ahead. The methodology took 20 years to build. |

---

## Anti-Persona (NOT Our Customer)

- Companies looking for someone to "just implement ChatGPT"
- Organizations where leadership isn't committed to change
- Teams that want a vendor recommendation, not a transformation strategy
- Solopreneurs or very small teams (under 20 employees)
- People who want a cheap AI course, not strategic consulting
